## 1. THE ORIGIN

**CHOPPED.** was born in an Austin alleyway at 02:00 AM. It was built for the **Unc** who still wants to look dangerous but needs to feel human.

Streetwear didn't age with us. While the culture chased neon logos and teenage hype, the OGs who built it were in the shop, the studio, and the streets, putting in the decades. They earned the "Black Air Force" energy — but their backs started keeping score. CHOPPED. exists to close that gap in the concrete.

We don't do "Dad Style." We do **Refined Chaos**: the silhouettes that defined our youth, re-engineered with industrial-grade textiles and medical-grade support.

---

## 2. THE PROBLEM & THE INSIGHT

**The problem:** Streetwear is engineered for the feed, not the body. The 38+ veteran who built the culture has no gear made for the physical reality of a life lived at 100% — back pain, long hours, real utility, real wear.

**The insight:** Back pain is the tax on a life lived at 100%. Gear should hold its shape as well as you do. We don't hide the grey; we wear it with all-black suede.

**The answer:** Function for the friction. Orthopedic support disguised as street icons. Armor, not fashion.

---

## 3. POSITIONING STATEMENT

> For the 38+ streetwear veteran whose body now pays the price for the grind, **CHOPPED.** is industrial-authority streetwear that re-engineers the icons of his youth with orthopedic support, heavyweight industrial textiles, and tactical utility — because unlike hype-driven, youth-chasing streetwear, we build armor for the physical reality of the night-shift legend.

## 4. TARGET AUDIENCE — "THE UNC"

- **Who:** 38+ streetwear veteran. Midnight-shift legend. Shop owner, studio head, mechanic, engineer, skater, creator, biker, musician.
- **What he earned:** 20+ years in the friction. The "Black Air Force" energy. Respect.
- **What he needs:** To still look dangerous while feeling human. Support for his stance. Capacity for his EDC (readers, fobs, tools). Gear that survives the Austin sun and the shop floor.
- **What he rejects:** Hype, cosplay, marketing fluff, seasonal churn, being called a "customer."
- **How he buys:** IYKYK. If he misses the 02:00 AM drop, the gear is gone. No begging, no discounts.

## 9. VOICE & TONE (SUMMARY)

*Full system: `choppedvoice.md` + `brand-voice-guidelines.md`.*

- **Syntax:** Max 10 words per sentence. Imperative mood. Periods only — never exclamation points.
- **Banned ("The Clutter"):** trendy, must-have, stylish, fashionable, customer, shopper, user, sale, discount, bargain, excited, amazing, game-changer, collection, season, launch.
- **Required ("The Chassis"):** Veteran, OG, The Unc, night-shift legend · Chassis, Armor, Gear, Hardware, Kit · Friction, Grind, Midnight, Support, Stance · Acquire, Secure, Deploy, Clock-in, Lockdown.

- **Components:** Square corners (no rounding), 1px borders, no box-shadows, no gradients. Inputs as terminal prompts (`> ENTER ZIP`).
- **Texture:** Film grain, noise, "cracked" overlays throughout.
- **Direction anchor:** Every pixel should read as if displayed on a scratched monitor inside a dark Austin garage at 02:00 AM.
- **Logo marks:** `CHOPPED.` wordmark · Stencil "C" · "Black Tape" DIY gaffer-tape treatment. *(Open item: no production vector/SVG master yet — see §15.)*

## 12. BRAND FICTION & MODEL (GUARDRAILS)

- **The Advisory Board:** No influencers. An Advisory Board of real 40+ men (mechanics, engineers, skaters, bikers, musicians) who "test the chassis," not model it. *Respect the veterans.*
- **Founders in-world:** Thomas Schustereit and Andy Smith are the real operators but appear **only as two Advisory Board figures** — never a direct-address founder bio. Do not break the Unc persona.
- **Scarcity ≠ inventory:** CHOPPED. holds no inventory (Printful POD dropship). The 02:00 AM drop and `[REDACTED]` language are a **timed ritual**, not a supply claim. Copy must never imply real stock limits ("only 3 left," "selling out"). *This is a QA guardrail — easy to violate accidentally.*
